Azure Databricks upgrade (Layer6 AI)
  • I was tasked to upgrade machine learing tool called Azure Databricks. The work involved not only myself provisioning the infrastructure but also other engineers redeploying lots of applications to use the new infrastructure.
  • I had to come up with a plan so that everyone in the team could work on the same time window to do the upgrade.
  • My manager was questioning me when the upgrade could be done because I was having hard time to get everyone agree on a time window. People had different priorities and schedules. The criticism from my manager was that because I was leading this effort, I must come up with a working plan to do this. I started sending out frequent communication over a chat. I invited all engineers into a chat and put the planned actions for the Databricks upgrade activity on a sticky note. And I created a table to show who is assigned to redeploy which application and when they are available to do the work. When a person could not find time to do it, I asked if another person could fill-in. And I promised to provide support when engineers face issues during the deployment.
  • Fortunately, I was able to complete the Databricks upgrade on time.

Past learnings

  • What stakeholders told me was rarely what they actually wanted. Requirements were never written in a way that delivered context. I realized I needed to interpret the requirements correctly by contiunously asking questions.
  • Mixing up agile and waterfall was a mistake because we had fixed deliverables by certain date while allowing new items to come into the sprint. And we end up with infinite amount of work. If some items come in, then some items need to move out.
  • Manual testing and deployment hit the limit quickly as the application became more complex. We insisted "due deligence" of human effort but we quickly realized that everyone makes mistakes. Automation is not only recommended, it is necessary.
  • When we were estimating the work effort, giving a range of time only made stakeholders assume the shortest. I learned to consider the worst case scenario to always make promise that I can deliver.
