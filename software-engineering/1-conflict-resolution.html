  • All levels
    • Clearly identify who was involved and what they wanted
    • Explain what caused the conflict at its core
    • Describe your specific role (participant, mediator, observer)
    • Demonstrate active listening and empathy
    • Explain how the conflict was resolved
    • Reflect on what you learned
  • Senior
    • Focus on strategic approaches to resolution
    • Demonstrate influence and consensus-building
    • Achieve measurable business outcomes
  • Staff
    • Address conflicts affecting multiple teams/departments
    • Influence senior stakeholders effectively
    • Identify and address systemic causes
    • Have an effective mental model of your counterparty
  • Manager
    • Focus on resolving interpersonal conflicts within your team and improving team dynamics
    • Help coach team members to navigate their own conflicts
    • Reach a conclusion which built trust and respect between parties while also keeping the organization's goals in mind

You disagreed with teammates or leaders

  • S
    • I was leading initiative to provision data platform for TD Securities in Nov 2025
    • While I was working to deploy the platform into production, my manager demaned that we open up DEV environment to users first
  • T
    • I disagreed with my manager because DEV environment was by nature unstable and we were only few days away from launching production
    • I suspected that there are non-technical reason (for example, politics) that made my manager to rush
  • A
    • I asked my manger and my team members to open up a call so that we can talk in person
    • I first heard my manager, and he advocated that users are urgently waiting for something that they can play with
    • I negotiated with my manager to let users know and accept that DEV is very unstable environment, and that they can lose their work anytime
    • We also agreed that my manager would advice users to switch over to production instance immediately after it becomes available
  • R
    • Both my manager's boss and his skip were happy that we delivered something to users by a fixed date, which goes into metrics showing entire team's performance
    • We still met internal deadline, which was very important, while still delievering robost product in production

You had to navigate competing priorities

  • S
    • Supporting Enterprise DevOps pipeline (TD Wealth SDDE) (Mar 2021)
    • Inner-sourcing in cloud engineering, working as a developer for the enterprise pipeline
    • Users were always complaining about pipeline support
    • Users didn't know how to use the pipeline
  • T
    • Had to to perform the role of not only developer but release engineer and technical support at the same time
  • A
    • Created a FAQ document that summarized common problems and solutions
    • First redirected user to FAQ document when they reported problems
    • Set priorities such that production technical support first, development second, and all others third
    • Advised stakeholders to investigate any non-production issues themselves before contacting myself
  • R
    • Usage rate of deployment pipeline increased about twice in the bank about a year after I join the team
    • Helping users learn how to use the tools rather than solving problems on their behalf wins in long run
