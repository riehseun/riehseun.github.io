Work

Publish

  • Have 1:1 with manager regularly
    • How did I deliver the expectation?
    • What do I need from my manager?
  • Have 1:1 with skip-level manager every few months
  • Present to the team when work is done
  • Ex. 1:1, presentation, knowledge-sharing sessions

Organize

  • Have 1:1 with product manager, engineering manager, stakeholder regularly
    • Find and talk to stalkholders that can block projects
    • Send regular update via email on progress (mildstone), risk, timeline change, confidence about deadlines
  • Attend to leadership's communication (all-hands, town halls)
  • Find and remove redundant or uncessary processes
  • Ex. discussion, planning, meeting, helping others, interviews

Produce

  • Find impactful work
  • Ship more and better than expected
  • When work is done, is must be properly done (watch for bugs, uncovered edge cases)
    • Have a written specification
    • Have a testing plan
    • Include testing, monitoring, and altering as part of estimation
  • Find cross-team projects
  • Ex. code change, code review, code release, design doc, troubleshooting

Weekly work log

  • Publish
    • Presentation, knowledge-sharing session
    • Meeting notes
  • Organize
    • Meeting notes
  • Produce

Onboarding

  • Clarify the work I will be doing
  • Understand one month, three months, six months goals (Understand team's priorities)
  • Ask how things work (Do not assume)
  • Find an onboarding buddy
  • Connect with peers and product folks
  • Introduce myself to other engineering teams
  • Draw a map of teams
  • Create cheatsheet (Acronyms, build commands, bookmarks)
  • Learn team's tech stack
  • Become productive on team's codebase

Mission

  • Always be humble
  • Be willing to hear what you don't want to hear
  • Do not be afraid of being viewed as the dumbest person in the team
  • Explore outside of your strength zone
  • Be honest about your weakness
  • Treat everyone (bosses and janiors alike) with respect
  • Ruthlessly protect your time
  • Do not pay attention to gossips and internal politics
  • Stand strongly against micro-management
  • Invest time with right people
  • Match actions with words
  • Avoid interrputing other people speaking
  • Show you are willing to work side by side
  • You cannot succeed without the support of others
  • Be confident to do the same work performed by other team members
  • Ask for inputs before sharing your own opinion

Organization

  • Horizon 1 - manage to value, focus on consistency, rules, compliance. Predictable and well-known operation model
  • Horizon 2 - new customers, adjacent market, different business model
  • Horizon 3 - manage to growth, focus on constant experiementation. Must break rules and processes governing Horizon 1
  • Left unchecked, Horizon 1 leaders can consume all resources of the company leaving nothing for Horizon 3

Hire

  • Define roles and responsibilities clearly
  • Talent and fit over skills and experience
  • First love rarely ends up in a good marriage. See enough candidates

Team

  • Locality and simplicity - systems must be loosely coupled, features should be delivered fast and independently
  • Focus, flow, and joy - work on small batches and get continual feedbacks rather than waiting for other people to get things done on our behalf
  • Improvement of daily work - if there is a choice between a new feature and developer productivity, the latter must always be chosen
  • Psychological safety - there must be no fear for speaking up about what I know and what I feel
  • Customer focus - the value and impact of our work on our customer must always be considerd

Define work

  • Exactly what needs to be done
  • Why does it need to be done
  • When does it need to be done
  • Who is the best person to do it
  • How well must it be done

Evaluation

  • Do you consistently perform at high level?
  • Do you understand how your role fits in the organization?
  • Do you challenge the status quo?
  • Do you show enthusiasm?
  • Do you show commitment to team's goal?

Leadership

  • Influence others to follow the lead, getting them to participate, and helping them improve
    • Laissez faire - hands-off management with limited communication but expects high accountability
    • Consultant - decision making involves the team members and weighting the options
    • Dictatorship
    • Micro-management
  • Light shall shine to people making most contribution to the team
  • Take account of skills and interest when doing the work
  • Build on strength rather than weakness
  • Focus on those who have ability and desire (Passion and ownership cannot be taught)
  • Speak to the team before making changes that will impact them
  • Set clear and measurable expectations, document everything, and provide on-going feedbacks
  • Be transparent on bad news

Feedback

  • Positive feedback
    • Call out good work
    • Be specific
  • Negative feedback
    • Give feedbak in person
    • Make it clear you are on their side
    • Make it clear it is just your observation, which they can ignore if they wish

Low attitude

  • Have conversation about "your" observation
  • Ask for their side of story
  • Come to a place for agreement
  • Set out future actions with deadline
  • Confirm the value of person and show commitment to help
