You made significant mistakes

  • S
    • I was working as a front-ed developer at Royal Bank of Canada in May 2016
    • I was developing JavaScript for SharePoint application
    • My team was following agile practice with two weeks sprint cadence
  • T
    • Businees were very strict about their requirement and very demainding
    • New requirements continuously came into the sprint
    • Business were assuming shorted time when we give them a ranged dates for the estimate
    • We took the items coming from business
  • A
    • We ended up with totally unmanagable work load
    • We had to cut the test to deliever all requirements
    • Bugs were released to prodiction, which made business further unhappy
    • We did not have time to automate tests, and manual testing came short as application grew
  • R
    • From then on, I made sure that story points for each sprint is fixed such that when new items come, equivalent workload is moved out
    • From then on, I started asking lots of questions when stakeholders deliever requirements to us
    • From then on, I started dedicating some sprints just working on developer productivity
    • From then on, I started under-promise and over-deliver by providing worst estimate
