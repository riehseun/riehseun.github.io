Residual block

  • To compute \( a^{[l+2]} \) using \( a^{[l]} \), the following computation needs to be performed.
  • \( z^{[l+1]} = W^{[l+1]}a^{[l]} + b^{[l+1]} \)
  • \( a^{[l+1]} = g(z^{[l+1]}) \)
  • \( z^{[l+2]} = W^{[l+2]}a^{[l+1]} + b^{[l+2]} \)
  • \( a^{[l+2]} = g(z^{[l+2]}) \)
  • Using skip connection, the last equation takes in additional information.
  • \( a^{[l+2]} = g(z^{[l+2]}+W_{s}a^{[l]}) \) where \( W_{s} \) is used to adjust the dimension of \( a^{[l]} \)

1x1 convolution

  • While pulling layers are used to shrink \( n_{H} \) and \( n_{W} \), 1x1 convoltion is used to shrink \( n_{c} \)
  • Ex. \( 6 \cdot 6 \cdot 32 \) * \( 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 32 \) = \( 6 \cdot 6 \cdot number of filters \)

Inception network

  • With \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \) input image, apply 3 different convolutions and 1 max-pooling.
    • \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \) image * \( 1 \cdot 1 \) = \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 64 \).
    • \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \) image * \( 3 \cdot 3 \)(same) = \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 128 \)
    • \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \) image * \( 5 \cdot 5 \) (same) = \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 32 \)
    • \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \) image * MAXPOOL (same, s=1) = \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 32 \)
  • Add the outputs to get the total output, which is \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 256 \)

Reduce computational overhead using 1x1 convolution

  • Input: \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 192 \)
    • Use ( 1 \cdot 1, 16, 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 192 \)
  • Bottleneck block: \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 16 \)
    • Use ( 5 \cdot 5, 32, 5 \cdot 5 \cdot 16 \)
  • \( 28 \cdot 28 \cdot 32 \)
